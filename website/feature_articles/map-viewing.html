OsmAnd gives you great possibilities for using the maps. You can customize them, download different types of maps and even edit them!

We recommend you to download the World basemap before proceeding. You can then move on to downloading the map of your region or the one you're planning to travel to. OsmAnd uses the OpenStreetMap, the maps made by the open-source project, a community of volunteer contributors who work on the maps every day. This is why they are so up-to-date. They're also quite compact and rapidly rendered as the basic OsmAnd map is a vector-type map, which means it carries vector representation of the map details rendered together on your device as opposed to the tile or raster maps that are 'pictures'.

Customize your map (Android)

As you open the map, you'll be able to customize its view in may different ways.

You can choose what exactly you'd like to see on the map in terms of additional details for each navigation profiles. To do that, just tap Menu-> Configure map. You can set all the needed parameters for your map. At this screen, there are two parts: Show and Map rendering.

In Show section, we configure the parameters for displaying them on the map.

In Map rendering section, we change the parameters that change map style.

Go to the Show section. There, you'll be able to choose:

• "Favorites": show Favorites on the map.

• "POI overlay...": show needed Points of interest (POI) on the map or find POI.

• "POI overlay labels": add labels of POI overlay.

• "Transport": show transport routes and stops.

• "GPX files...": choose and show GPX tracks on the map.

• "Map markers": show Map markers on the map.

• "Map source...": choose data of the main map (Offline vector maps by default).

• "Mapillary": viewing images of streets by Mapillary service.

• "Overlay and Underlay map...": show added layers of the map, needed special plugin.

• "Recording layer": show your recording (audio/video/photo) notes on the map, needed special plugin.

• "Contour lines" and "Hillshades layer": show contour lines and hillshades on the map, needed special plugin.

• "OSM notes (online)" and "OSM edits": show OpenStreetMap edits and notes on the map, needed special plugin.

• "OsmAnd Tracker": show your sharing contacts from OsmAnd Tracker on the map, needed installation third-party app.

Go to Map rendering section. There, you'll be able to choose:

• "Map style": choose your style for the map.

• "Map mode": set day/night mode for map and application. You can see information about Sunset or Sunrise in the second line of Map mode.

The night mode has a darker layout and low brightness in general. This is made to help you avoid eye strain. The day mode is set by default: it is your OsmAnd map the way you see it when you launch the application.The Sunrise/Sunset mode changes the color scheme automatically based on the time set on your device. The Light sensor option turns the night and day mode on based on the lighting in your environment. Light sensor option works on phones that have a light sensor on the front. When you get to a dark place, be it a night outdoors or a dark room, the app will switch to the night map viewing mode. As you get to a lighted area, it'll change the map viewing mode to Day.

• "Map magnifier": choose scale for your map. You can change it to long click to "+" or "-" on the map.

• "Road style": change style of roads on the map (Default, German road atlas, American road atlas, High contrast roads, Bold outline).

• "Text size": set text size on the map.

• "Map language": choose language for text on the map (Local name by default). You can set "Transliterate if "Language" name is missing".

• "Details": show any details on the map - More details, Show road surface, Show road quality, Show access restrictions and toll, Show Low Emission Zones, Colour-code buildings by type, Street lighting, OSM mapper assistant, Nautical depth contours.

• "Hide": hide any details on the map - Boundaries, Polygons, Buildings, Hide water, House numbers, Proposed objects, POI icons, POI labels, Underground objects, Overground objects.

• "Routes": show special routes - Show cycle routes, Show node network cycle routes, Alpine hiking scale (SAC), Horse routes, Whitewater sports).

You can switch on Hiking symbol overlay: Disabled, Colouring according to OSMC, Colouring according to route scope, Node networks.

Customize your map (iOS)

You can change the map to make app use as comfortable as possible. To do that, tap app menu-> Map.

There are five sections of this menu: Show on Map, Map type, Map style, Overlay / Underlay, Map Language.

In the section Show on map, you can choose items for shoowing them on the map:

• "Favorites": show Favorites on the map.

• "POI overlay": show needed Points of interest (POI) on the map or find POI.

• "OSM Edits/Notes (offline)" and "OSM notes (online)": show OpenStreetMap edits and notes on the map, needed special plugin.

• "Mapillary": viewing images of streets by Mapillary service.

• "Trips": choose and show GPX tracks on the map.

In the section Map type, you can choose preferred type of the map. More information about Map style you can read here.

In the section Map style, you can choose many parameters for changing the map:

• "Application mode": set day/night mode for map and application.

The night mode has a darker layout and low brightness in general. This is made to help you avoid eye strain. The day mode is set by default: it is your OsmAnd map the way you see it when you launch the application.The Sunrise/Sunset mode changes the color scheme automatically based on the time set on your device.

• "Map magnifier": choose scale for your map.

• "Text size": set text size on the map.

• "Details": show any details on the map - More details, Show road surface, Show road quality, Show access restrictions and toll, Show Low Emission Zones, Colour-code buildings by type, Street lighting, OSM mapper assistant, Nautical depth contours.

• "Hide": hide any details on the map - Boundaries, Polygons, Buildings, Hide water, House numbers, Proposed objects, POI icons, POI labels, Underground objects, Overground objects.

• "Routes": show special routes - Show cycle routes, Show node network cycle routes, Alpine hiking scale (SAC), Horse routes, Whitewater sports).

You can switch on Hiking symbol overlay: Disabled, Colouring according to OSMC, Colouring according to route scope, Node networks.

• "Transport": show transport routes and stops.

• "Road style": change style of roads on the map (Default, German road atlas, American road atlas, High contrast roads, Bold outline).

• "Contour lines": show contour lines on the map, needed special plugin.

In the section Overlay/Underlay, you can set layers for the map. For example, use a Bing Earth map as an underlay and add a hillshade overlay. To change the transparency of the layer, just drag the transparency slider.

• "Hillshade": show contour lines on the map, needed special plugin.

• "Overlay and Underlay": show added layers of the map, needed special plugin.

In the section Map Language, you can choose language for text on the map (Local name by default). You can set "Transliterate if "Language" name is missing". Please note that the Map language sets the language of the names on the map.

Viewing widgets (Android)

You can see many information on your screen except tha map: widgets, compass, map scale, app buttons.

OsmAnd offers a wide range of widgets to help you see exactly what you need during navigation and when you just view the map. Of course, you can click to widgets to change the displayed information.

For configuration wigets you should go to app menu-> Configuration screen.

Note: you can switch on Plugins: (app menu-> Plugins) for dislayed some of widgets.

You can also switch to view the dashboard at the start. A dashboard is a starting screen with different cards containing information about different groups of settings or files. The dashboard shows your Favourites (favorite places), your GPX tracks list, the plugin's information, etc. To adjust your dashboard settings, please go to OsmAnd - Dashboard and press the settings button in the upper-right corner.

OsmAnd offers a wide range of widgets to help you see exactly what you need during navigation and when you just view the map.To select the widgets you need, please go to Configure screen menu. You can select speed widget to control your movement, view altitude, speed limit, radius ruler etc. Also, some plugins have widgets to help you operate them. For example, the audio/video notes plugin has a widget to help you make a note in one tap. As you enable or disable a plugin, the widgets will turn on and off. Also, you can turn the widgets on or off without disabling the plugin. Just go to Configure screen — and check or uncheck the boxes with the widgets of your choice.

Viewing widgets (iOS)

In iOS, you need to turn on the plugin to view the widget. Please go to Maps & Resources and switch to plugins using the button below, after that switch the plugin you need on.
