OsmAnd Contour lines plugin

July 15, 2015

OsmAnd Nautical Charts is a plugin to OsmAnd Maps and Navigation app called to make navigation on water easier. It gives detailed information for using any vehicle on water including the ares that are allowed and not allowed to sail in, dangerous areas, sailing routes, harbors, etc.

How to use it

To see the contours of a specific area, just go to map downloads, choose Contour lines in the upper menu bar (it is set to Regular maps by default) and get the region you need. Then zoom your desired area until you see the lines and zoom even more to check the height of a specific spot (the number on the line).

To see the visualization of a terrain, make sure you download the corresponding section of the map (switch to Hillshade overlay in the All downloads menu) and enable Hillshade in your map configurations, then relaunch the app.

After that, you'll be able to enjoy a map with all the hills and peaks. A nice option for both practical use and a general knowledge about the area you live in or travel to.

Contour lines plugin is a handy tool for hikers, tourists and anyone who needs the information about the terrain in a specific area.

The global data (between 70 degrees north and 70 degrees south) is based on measurements by SRTM (Shuttle Radar Topography Mission) and ASTER (Advanced Spaceborne Thermal Emission and Reflection Radiometer), an imaging instrument onboard Terra, the flagship satellite of NASA's Earth Observing System. ASTER is a cooperative effort between NASA, Japan's Ministry of Economy, Trade and Industry (METI), and Japan Space Systems (J-spacesystems).
